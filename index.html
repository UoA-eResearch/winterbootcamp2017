About

Winter Bootcamp is a week long training and education event co-ordinated by the Center for eResearch where you can up-skill in a whole host of topics related to digital research practice. Workshops and seminars range from two-day intensive and hands-on Software Carpentry programmes for general audiences, through to short informational or networking sessions. Included are also a number of specialised offerings in topics such as high performance computing and bioinformatics. Browse the menu below to see what's on and register for sessions. Places are limited so don't delay.

Workshop hosts include the Centre for eResearch, Libraries and Learning Services, New Zealand eScience Infrastructure, the Bioinformatics Institute, New Zealand Genomics Ltd., the Centre for Computational Evolution, and Biomatters.

Research Data Management

Why? Research funders and journal publishers increasingly require researchers to make their research data available for discovery and reuse. University guidance states that research data be stored for a minimum of 6 years. Effective management of your research data reduces loss, saves time, facilitates collaboration and increases the impact of your research.

The University of Auckland is developing Research Data Management (RDM) services to meet these increasing requirements, a collaboration between Libraries and Learning Services and the Centre for eResearch.

Who? Aimed at all staff researchers and PhD candidates interested in storing, sharing and publishing their research data and increasing the impact of their research.

What? Workshop provides an introduction to research data management concepts and the support and services at the University of Auckland, including demonstration of the University's Data Publishing and Discovery Service - auckland.figshare.com Attendees will develop strategies for capturing and organising research data, explore options around publishing, sharing and reusing data, and start to draft a Data Management Plan.

  • Hosted by: Centre for eResearch and Libraries and Learning Services
  • Date: July 13th
  • Time: 10:00 - 12:00 rm 303-B05

Genome Assembly, Annotation and Visualisation


Understanding and using genomic data is of critical importance. In this one-day workshop, you will:

  • Assemble a small genome de novo, and understand the process of assembly and quality assessment of genomes.
  • Generate annotations by predicting genes and genomic features in a genome, and understand the theory and limitations of gene prediction.
  • Understand genome annotations, and visualise and interpret your annotations in a genome browser.
  • Discuss new technologies (e.g long-read and single-molecule sequencing, single-cell genomics) and their uses and limitations.

  • Time: 9:00 - 17:00 rm 303-B07

Transcriptomics


Transcriptomics (using Next-Generation Sequencing) is a fast-moving area which continues to see developments in data analysis, experimental design, and sequencing technologies. In this one-day workshop, you will:

  • Design and analyse an RNA-seq experiment (both with and without a reference genome) using current versions of the Tuxedo and Trinity workflows.
  • Use ERCC spike-in controls and the ercc dashboard to define the lower limit of detection, dynamic range of your experiment, and to detect any biases introduced during the experiment.
  • Visualise your results, both at the whole-transcriptome level and at the level of the individual gene.
  • Discuss emerging sequencing technologies (e.g. molecular indexing, single-cell transcriptomics, long-read technologies) that can add significant value to your experimental design, and the state of bioinformatic tools used to exploit these technologies.

  • Hosted by: Bioinformatics Institute and NZGL
  • Date: July 14th
  • Time: 9:00 - 17:00 rm 303-B07

Metabarcoding


Metabarcoding allows for rapid analysis of the species diversity present in populations. In this one-day workshop, you will:

  • Understand the use of positive and negative controls and mock communities.
  • Demultiplex samples using fastq_multx (if samples contain more than one amplicon).
  • Ensure that reads are properly paired using SolexaQA++ lengthsort.
  • Generate OTU tables and assign taxonomy using VSEARCH and QIIME.
  • Use QIIME to calculate diversity within and between samples and visualise the results.

  • Hosted by: Bioinformatics Institute and NZGL
  • Date: July 15th
  • Time: 9:00 - 17:00 rm 303-B07

Computational phylogenetics using BEAST 2


Bayesian computational methods have transformed the study of evolutionary processes, yielding deep quantitative insights into the ancestral histories of genes, populations and species. This workshop will be composed of two parts, the first of which will bring participants up to speed on the statistical theory behind these methods. The second part will be a tutorial during which participants will learn to infer phylogenetic trees and ancestral population dynamics from real genetic data using the BEAST 2 software package

  • Hosted by: Centre for Computational Evolution
  • Date: July 15th
  • Time: 13:00 - 16:00 rm 303-B11
